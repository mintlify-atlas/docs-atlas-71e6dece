Once you are signed in, you can establish your first VPN connection in seconds.

## Connect with one tap

The fastest way to connect is to let Proton VPN select the optimal server for you.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Proton VPN">
    Launch the app. You will land on the home screen showing your current connection state.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap the Connect button">
    Tap the large **Connect** button in the center of the home screen. Proton VPN automatically selects the fastest available server based on your location.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Grant VPN permission">
    The first time you connect, Android will ask for permission to set up a VPN connection. Tap **OK** to allow it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for the connection">
    The app will show a **Connecting** state briefly, then transition to **Connected** once the tunnel is established.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  The fastest server selection prioritizes low latency. If you want to connect to a specific country or city, see [Choosing a country or server](#choosing-a-country-or-server) below.
</Tip>

## Choosing a country or server

You can browse and connect to servers by country or choose a specific server.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the server list">
    From the home screen, tap **Countries** or swipe up to open the full server list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a country">
    Tap a country to expand it and see the available server groups or individual servers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect">
    Tap a country name to connect to the fastest server in that country, or tap a specific server to connect to it directly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Free plan accounts have access to servers in a limited set of countries. Servers marked with a lock icon require a paid plan. See [account setup](/getting-started/account-setup) for plan details.
</Note>

## Understanding connection states

The home screen reflects your current connection status at all times.

| State | What it means |
|-------|--------------|
| **Disconnected** | No VPN tunnel is active. Your traffic is going through your regular internet connection. |
| **Connecting** | Proton VPN is negotiating the VPN tunnel. This usually takes a few seconds. |
| **Connected** | The VPN tunnel is active. Your traffic is encrypted and routed through the selected server. |

When connected, the home screen shows the server you are connected to, your new IP address, and the VPN protocol in use.

## Disconnect

To end your VPN session:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap Disconnect">
    On the home screen, tap the **Disconnect** button. The tunnel closes immediately and your traffic returns to your regular connection.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Disconnecting from the VPN does not sign you out. Your account remains active and you can reconnect at any time.
</Warning>

## Quick Settings tile

Proton VPN provides a Quick Settings tile so you can connect and disconnect without opening the app.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Quick Settings">
    Swipe down from the top of your screen to open the notification shade, then swipe down again to reveal the full Quick Settings panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add the Proton VPN tile">
    Tap the edit (pencil) icon to enter tile editing mode. Find the **Proton VPN** tile and drag it into your active tiles.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use the tile">
    Tap the **Proton VPN** tile to toggle the VPN connection on or off. Long-pressing the tile opens the Proton VPN app directly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  The Quick Settings tile connects to the same server or location you last used, making it convenient for your usual connection.
</Tip>

## Home screen widget

Proton VPN also offers a home screen widget for one-tap access from your launcher.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Long-press your home screen">
    On your Android home screen, long-press an empty area to enter widget mode.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the Proton VPN widget">
    Tap **Widgets** and scroll until you find the **Proton VPN** widget. Multiple sizes are available.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Place the widget">
    Drag the widget to your preferred location on the home screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect from the widget">
    Tap the widget to connect or disconnect. The widget displays your current connection state and updates in real time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Widget availability may vary depending on your Android launcher. If you do not see the widget option, check that your launcher supports widgets.
</Note>
